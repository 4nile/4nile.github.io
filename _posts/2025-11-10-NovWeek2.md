<h1>In the literature last week</h1>
<h2>ArXiv fights scientific malpractices by banning compsci reviews</h2>
[link](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-03664-7){:.button.button--primary.button--pill}

In a context of [intense paper-milling and young research often self-citing to increase their indexation](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-024-04006-9) caused partially by the hyper-competitive industry that is academic research (a sentence that would be an oxymoron if not for the current grant-based system), arXiv moderators in the field of computer sciences have noticed a proliferation of so-called reviews that end up being bibliographical lists with not much added content from the author, no annotation or explanation of the link between the cited papers. Suspecting citation purchase and untraceable plagiarism caused by AIs, they have chosen to forbid the submission of reviews. Conversely, in parallel a [first ever conference has been organized with abstract and review performed only by AIs](https://www.science.org/content/article/futuristic-meeting-ais-took-lead-producing-and-reviewing-all-studies). Hopefully, this conference can lead to acknowledge in details the limitations of these tools, the first one in the question of reviewing being that LLMs tend to be programmed to be pleasing and access requests, making them mediocre critics of a paper's quality.

<h2>Ghostwriting for private companies and its consequences on the trustworthiness of research</h2>
[link](https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/science.aec4187?af=R){:.button.button--primary.button--pill}

> N. Oreskes, _Science_ 309 (6772), 2025

Glyphosates are a well-known scientific scandal in France, with still heavy debates as to whether or not they are truly dangerous for health. Orsekes and co-authors studied the impact of a paper that was recently revealed to have been ghostwritten for Monsanto with no independently acquired data, and showed that its influence was huge over the press and public opinion. Another example is studies by British pharmaceutical companies, which are heavily ghostwritten, with sometimes dramatic consequences such as avoidable deaths being caused by critical data being hidden by the brands to the researchers. The issue of ghostwriting however seems to be largely underestimated by the scientific community, whereas it drives a deep distrust from the general population, leading to an ever increased influence of pseudo-sciences and a rise of the anti intellectual sentiment.

<h2>Shear-induced bubble nucleation in magmas</h2>
[link](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adw8543#con1){:.button.button--primary.button--pill}

> Roche et al., _Science_ 390 (6773), 2025

An experiement is developed to model the bubble nucleation in magma below volcanoes, using PEO. The nucleation of a first bubble creates a mechanical shock leading to a homogeneous nucleation cascade. The conclusion of the paper is that shear-induced nucleation can happen at much lower Poiseuille number than expected.
